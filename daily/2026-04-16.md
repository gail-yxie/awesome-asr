# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-04-16

## Summary

Today's research highlight is the introduction of "audio-side time prompts" for Large Audio-Language Models (LALMs), a method that significantly enhances fine-grained temporal perception and event localization by bridging the modality gap in onset/offset detection. On the model front, the rapid adoption of *whisper-large-v3-turbo* is evident through several new quantized releases (GGUF, CTranslate2) and specialized fine-tunings, including niche applications like Swiss German fire brigade communications and Japanese anime. Additionally, the release of *parakeet-tdt-0.6b-portuguese* and *Audio2Phoneme-MA-french-V2* reflects a sustained focus on robust, non-English speech representations and downstream phonetic tasks. Collectively, these developments signal a shift from general audio understanding toward precise temporal grounding and high-efficiency, domain-specific deployment.

## Key Ideas


- The researchers introduce the Audio-Side Time Prompt (ATP) to inject precise temporal information directly into the audio representation, addressing a major bottleneck in Large Audio-Language Models.

- A novel post-training framework is proposed that enables existing LALMs to gain fine-grained temporal perception without requiring massive retraining of the entire architecture.

- Integrating time-aware tokens at the audio encoder's output helps the model bridge the gap between high-level semantic audio understanding and precise temporal grounding.

- Experimental results show that this approach significantly improves the model's ability to infer accurate onset and offset times for various sound events.

- This breakthrough allows Large Audio-Language Models to handle more practical, fine-grained tasks such as sound event localization and complex temporal reasoning.
