# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-03-21

## Summary

Today’s releases emphasize the continued push for inference optimization and specialized adaptation of the Whisper architecture, notably with new CTranslate2 conversions of distilled Whisper Large v3 to support high-throughput deployment in fp16 and bf16 precisions. Research in clinical ASR is highlighted by a LoRA-based fine-tune for dysarthric speech using the TORGO dataset, demonstrating a trend toward parameter-efficient adaptation for non-standard acoustic profiles. Additionally, the ecosystem saw a diversification of language support with new fine-tunes for Quechua, Luganda, and Mongolian, alongside a "small" Zipformer variant from the Icefall framework optimized for low-latency streaming applications. Collectively, these developments reflect a community focus on "deployment-readiness," bridging the gap between large foundational models and hardware-specific or niche-language requirements.
