# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-03-26

## Summary

Today's research highlights a significant focus on mitigating "contextual exposure bias" in Speech-LLMs by addressing the train-test mismatch between oracle training history and noisy inference context, a crucial step for robust conversational agents. Efforts to scale general audio understanding are also accelerating with the release of ACAVCaps, which provides the granular descriptions necessary for fine-grained audio-language modeling. The introduction of the Ethio-ASR suite and sociolinguistic analyses of Newcastle English further signal a growing industry-wide commitment to improving performance in low-resource languages and diverse dialectal variations. On the model front, activity is centered on fine-tuning Whisper-large-v3-turbo and Wav2Vec2 for regional applications, including Bengali and Vietnamese telephony, demonstrating a trend toward specialized, context-aware speech intelligence.

## Key Ideas


- Current ASR systems remain significantly biased against speakers with dialectal variations, such as Newcastle English, highlighting a critical gap in linguistic inclusivity within mainstream training datasets.

- The ACAVCaps dataset enables more robust audio-language models by providing the descriptive scale and granularity necessary for complex, diverse audio understanding tasks.

- Addressing contextual exposure bias through a unified training framework allows Speech-LLMs to maintain performance during inference despite the presence of noisy or error-prone conversational histories.

- Multi-layer contrastive supervision improves the reliability of Speech Emotion Recognition systems in real-world scenarios by better aligning representations between acted training data and spontaneous natural speech.

- Jointly training ASR and Language Identification for Afroasiatic languages demonstrates that cross-lingual knowledge sharing can significantly boost recognition accuracy for low-resource Ethiopian dialects.
