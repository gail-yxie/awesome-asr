# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-03-13

## Summary

Recent developments highlight a push toward real-time, deployment-ready Large Audio Language Models (LALMs), with **Uni-ASR** and **Hikari** introducing unified streaming architectures and policy-free causal alignment to mitigate the latency inherent in auto-regressive decoding. There is a growing research focus on multi-dimensional speech understanding, evidenced by techniques to resurface latent paralinguistic cues and the **Resonate** framework, which applies online RL feedback to improve text-to-audio generation quality. Specialized ASR is also advancing through biomarker-supervised models for pathological speech and state-of-the-art results for low-resource languages like Swahili via pseudo-labeled continued pretraining. Notable model releases include **GigaAM-v3** optimized for MLX and several niche **Whisper** variants, signaling a trend toward localizing and optimizing foundational models for specific linguistic contexts and edge hardware.

## Key Ideas


- Large Audio Language Models are evolving to incorporate paralinguistic awareness, moving beyond text-centric paradigms to capture subtle emotional and contextual cues in speech.

- Online feedback from Large Audio Language Models provides a more effective reinforcement learning signal for text-to-audio generation than traditional offline preference optimization.

- Causal alignment allows for the development of policy-free, end-to-end models capable of simultaneous speech-to-text translation and streaming transcription without complex heuristics.

- Speculative decoding facilitates the deployment of complex speech emotion captioning on resource-constrained edge devices by optimizing the collaboration between edge and cloud architectures.

- Pseudo-labeled continued pretraining effectively bridges the gap for low-resource languages, reaching state-of-the-art ASR performance with minimal human-labeled data.

- Incorporating clinical biomarkers as a supervision signal directly improves ASR performance on pathological speech by accounting for irregular timing and articulatory distortion.

- Unified architectures now allow Large Language Models to perform both high-accuracy non-streaming and low-latency streaming speech recognition within a single framework.
