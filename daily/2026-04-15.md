# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-04-15

## Summary

Today’s research shifts the focus of Large Audio-Language Models (LALMs) from basic perception to sophisticated reasoning and temporal precision, highlighted by **Audio-Cogito’s** implementation of Chain-of-Thought for audio and **SpotSound’s** framework for fine-grained event grounding in long-form recordings. Significant progress in real-time reliability is seen with **MoshiRAG**, which introduces asynchronous knowledge retrieval to full-duplex speech-to-speech models to address factuality during live interactions without compromising latency. Concurrently, the emergence of **timing-aware pre-quantization fusion** for audio-visual tokenization reflects a growing trend toward multimodal integration at the discrete representation level, complemented by the release of efficient edge-ready models like **Qwen3-ASR-1.7B** and domain-specific **Parakeet** variants.

## Key Ideas


- SpotSound introduces fine-grained temporal grounding to Large Audio-Language Models, enabling them to precisely pinpoint specific events within long-form audio.

- MoshiRAG enables full-duplex speech-to-speech models to maintain fluid, real-time interactivity while significantly improving factual accuracy through asynchronous knowledge retrieval.

- Audio-Cogito addresses the reasoning gap in Large Audio Language Models by integrating structured Chain-of-Thought capabilities for complex audio scene analysis.

- A new timing-aware pre-quantization fusion technique overcomes the limitations of single-modal audio tokenizers by leveraging video-enhanced discrete representations.

- Implementing asynchronous RAG frameworks allows conversational speech models to process pauses and interruptions while concurrently accessing external knowledge bases.

- Incorporating multimodal inputs before the quantization stage facilitates superior cross-modal alignment for end-to-end audio understanding and generation.
