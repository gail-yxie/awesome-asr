# ASR & Speech Language Daily Update — 2026-04-14

## Summary

Today’s research highlights a shift from basic Speech Language Model (SLM) capability toward emotional and expressive depth, evidenced by the HumDial-EIBench for multi-turn emotional intelligence evaluation and new "Self-Aware SLMs" designed to bridge the gap between semantic intent and acoustic realization. The release of Audio Flamingo Next advances open-source foundational models for holistic reasoning across speech, music, and environment sounds, while work on Ti-Audio and cross-lingual training strategies focuses on scaling these architectures to low-resource and multi-dialectal contexts. Architecturally, the adaptation of Granite-speech for Speaker Attributed ASR (SAA) underscores a trend toward using speech-aware LLMs to unify transcription and diarization into a single end-to-end task. Complementing these advancements, new model releases continue to prioritize efficient inference, with multiple ONNX and CTranslate2 optimizations of the Whisper and Parakeet families appearing for deployment-focused workflows.

## Key Ideas


- HumDial-EIBench establishes a more realistic evaluation for emotional intelligence by replacing synthetic, single-turn datasets with human-recorded, multi-turn dialogues.

- The "semantic understanding-acoustic realization gap" is identified as a primary cause of flat speech in models, requiring new self-aware architectures to ensure expressive intent matches semantic content.

- Speech-aware Large Language Models can perform integrated Speaker-Attributed ASR by learning to interleave relative speaker identity tags directly within the generated transcript.

- Ti-Audio proves that end-to-end speech LLMs can be successfully adapted to low-resource and multi-dialectal environments such as Tibetan despite severe data scarcity.

- New training strategies for cross-lingual speech LLMs offer a path to expand end-to-end audio understanding beyond English through more efficient data utilization and model expansion techniques.

- Audio Flamingo Next demonstrates that scaling foundation models and refining architectures enables a single model to excel at complex reasoning across speech, environmental sounds, and music.
